Section 3 Fun Notes

Manitoba Act of 1870

In 1870 the Manitoba Act was signed after Louis Riel's Provisional government showed the government a bill of rights. This established the 5th Province of Canada called Manitoba.

PEI joins Confederation

Prince Edward Island joins the Confederation and becomes the 7th Province. They got railroad funding, 6MPs, annual payments and land back from absentee British landowners.

Creation of the Northwest Mounted Police

In 1873 The Northwest Mounted Police was established to enforce law in the Northwest Territories. They would force whiskey traders out, deliver mail, and fight fires.

Northwest Resistance (2nd MÃ©tis Uprising)

In 1884 The Northwest Resistance took place. Louis Riel and Gabriel Dumont were involved. They battled at Batoche for 3 days before surrendering. Louis Riel was hanged the next year.

Manitoba Schools Act

In 1890 people of Manitoba voted to remove French from the government of Manitoba. The Manitoba Schools Act was signed as a result. Now English is the only language spoken in the Assembly and used to write laws.

The Wright Brothers Invent the First Airplane

In 1903 Wilbur and Orville Wright made the first airplane. Their invention would show up in WW1. Passenger planes would be manufactured as well.

Alberta and Saskatchewan joins Confederation

In 1905 The Alberta Act and Saskatchewan Act added two more provinces to the Confederation. They were established so leaders in the Northwest territories could collect taxes.

World War 1

WW1 started in 1914 when Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated. Canada joined the war the very same year.

Battle of Vimy Ridge

The Battle of Vimy Ridge was in 1917. This battle has importance to Canadian Identity since the international community sees Canada as more than a British colony. The reasons for this are the huge loss of life, and acts committed by Canadian soldiers.

Women Gains Suffrage

In 1918 most women gained the right to vote federally, but it wouldn't be until 1960 where women of all races could vote with the addition of Indigenous populations.

Winnipeg General Strike

In 1919 a 40 day general strike happened in Winnipeg where 30,000 workers took to the streets to rally for better working conditions. In the end the NWMP apprehended the strikers, and hit them with bats.

Chinese Immigration Act

In 1923 The Chinese Immigration Act was passed to completely stop the flow of Chinese Immigrants. They established this after seeing that the Head Tax had failed to deter Chinese Immigrants.

"Persons" Case

Emily Murphy and 4 other women took the "persons" case to the Supreme Court to earn the right for women to participate in the Senate. The supreme court ruled that women weren't persons in 1928, but they took it to a higher court in England in 1929, but this time they won.

The Great Depression

The stock market crashed in 1929, and Canada went into an economic depression. As a result, people were unemployed. The prairies were hit hard, since they were in a drought which resulted in a dust storm.

The CBC is founded.

In 1936 the Canadian Government created the CBC to distribute Canadian news and content over radio. At the time most radio stations came from the US.

World War 2

In 1939 Germany started World War 2, and Canada fought in it. The war brought Canada out of the Great Depression, and Canada helped capture French beaches.

Newfoundland Joins Confederation

Newfoundland and Labrador joined Canada in 1949 and became the 10th and final province in the Confederation. Before joining, the British colony held a referendum where the majority voted to join Canada.

Offical Langauges Act

In 1969, The Official Languages Act caused the government to become Bilingual. Laws were written in English and French, and government services can be received in either language.

Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms

The Charter of Rights and Freedoms was passed in 1982 which guaranteed every Canadian basic rights and Freedoms. The Charter also further enforced Bilingualism with a section about language rights.
